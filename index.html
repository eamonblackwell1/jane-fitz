Your Vision

You want something real. Not a production, not a performance. A wedding surrounded by the people who matter most, set against the kind of scenery that makes everyone go quiet for a second. Mt. Fuji in the background. Nature everywhere. The kind of day where Fitz can actually be present, not running on someone else's schedule, and where you both feel like yourselves.

You fell in love with Japan together. Four cities in two weeks. You already know what it feels like to be there. Now you want to bring your closest people into that feeling: the calm, the beauty, the food, the little moments that make Japan unlike anywhere else. Twelve of you, ten days, no rush.

This isn't about choosing between a big wedding at home and a small one far away. It's about choosing the version that feels most like you. We think Japan is that version, and we want to show you exactly what it could look like.

Day One

Arrival in Tokyo

Your group lands in Tokyo. After the long haul from Perth, the priority is simple: get settled, decompress, and soak it in. We'll arrange airport transfers and get everyone to their accommodation in Shinjuku or Shibuya, close to transport links and within walking distance of good food.

No agenda tonight. Just wander, eat, sleep. If some of your group have been to Japan before, they'll know the drill. For first-timers, this is their introduction. Let the jet lag sort itself out over a bowl of ramen and a cold beer.

Day Two

Welcome Dinner

This is the day your wedding trip officially begins. Everyone has arrived at different times, found their feet, and now — for the first time — your whole group sits down together. Walk through the week ahead, let the excitement settle in, and let the night take over.

We'll book a welcome dinner at a spot that sets the tone: an izakaya with a private room, or a yakitori restaurant where everyone can share plates and conversation. It's the first meal together in Japan. That energy matters.

Day Three

Tokyo, Your Way

A free day in Tokyo for the group to explore. You've been here before, so you know the rhythm. For your guests who haven't, we'll put together a curated guide with our picks based on what they're into: food markets, temples, shopping, gardens, arcades, whatever fits.

During our discovery conversation, we'll learn what your group has and hasn't done in Japan, and we'll tailor suggestions accordingly. If there's a specific experience you want to organize as a group (a cooking class, a sake tasting, a day trip to Kamakura), we can build that in.

This is also the last night in Tokyo, so if there's a restaurant you've been dreaming about, this is the night to book it.

Day Four

Journey to Mt. Fuji

The group travels from Tokyo to the Mt. Fuji area. It's about two hours by highway bus from Shinjuku, or we can arrange a private transfer for a more relaxed ride. The scenery shifts from city to countryside, and then Fuji appears.

Check into your ceremony venue accommodation. The rest of the day is about settling in and soaking up the setting. If you're at a ryokan, that means hot springs, a kaiseki dinner, and the kind of quiet that's hard to find anywhere else. For you and Fitz, this is also the afternoon for any final touches: dress fitting, running through the ceremony flow, making sure everything feels right.

No pressure. Just presence.

Day Five

Your Wedding Day

This is it. You wake up at the foot of Mt. Fuji, surrounded by the people you love most. The morning is yours: get ready together, take your time, look out the window at that view.

The ceremony happens in a setting that's all nature. No church. No banquet hall. Just you, Fitz, your 10 guests, and the mountain. The reception flows naturally into a dinner together: a long table, incredible food, and the kind of conversation that only happens when you put the right people in a beautiful place.

The venue options below each offer a different version of this day. All of them put Mt. Fuji front and center. All of them include what matters: the ceremony, the dinner, accommodation for the night, and a photographer to capture it.

Ceremony & Venue Options

All-Inclusive

The Grand Ryokan

A prestigious 5-star onsen ryokan with a 66,000 sqm Japanese garden and direct Mt. Fuji views. This is the turnkey option: ceremony, reception dinner with drinks, dress and tux, bouquet, cake, one night's stay for all 12 guests, and a photographer are all included. Intimate banquet hall seats up to 26. Hot springs, drum shows, and traditional hospitality round out the experience. Approx. 9,000 AUD for 12 guests.

Creative & Private

The Forest Studio

A converted photography studio on a 3,100 sqm estate in the forest at the base of Mt. Fuji. One group per day, completely private. Three bedrooms, two living rooms, a full kitchen, outdoor BBQ deck, and garden terrace. The space is raw, architectural, and surrounded by forest. You'd bring in your own catering (BBQ or private chef) and decorations, giving you total creative freedom. Note: can't see Mt. Fuji from this venue, but the forest setting is stunning.

Luxury Glamping

The Highland Retreat

A luxury villa resort at the foot of Mt. Fuji with direct mountain views. Private villa-style rooms with spacious terraces, natural hot springs, a Finnish sauna, and outdoor firepit areas. BBQ dinner under the stars with locally sourced ingredients from the Fuji foothills. The vibe is refined outdoor living: nature forward, but with hotel-level comfort. Multiple villa units can accommodate the full group across the property.

Intimate & Minimal

The Private Farmhouse

A single-building private hotel for one group per day, set on farmland near Lake Kawaguchi. Every window frames Mt. Fuji. Finnish sauna, cold plunge using natural Mt. Fuji spring water, and a full kitchen for catering or self-cooking. The design is minimal and intentional: clean lines, natural materials, total quiet. This is the option for couples who want nothing between them and the mountain. Accommodates one group only.

Day Six

Breathe, Then Move On

No alarms. Sleep in. Soak in the hot springs one more time. Have breakfast looking out at the mountain and let the previous day sink in.

After checkout, the group travels to your second destination. This is where the trip shifts from wedding to holiday, and you have two options for how those final days unfold.

Days Seven to Nine

Your Second Chapter

After the wedding, you have three full days to explore together before heading home. We see two directions this could go, and both are great. It depends on what feels right for you, Fitz, and your group.

Scenario A

Return to Kyoto

You've been to Kyoto before, but your guests likely haven't. And honestly, Kyoto is the kind of place that reveals more every time you go. This time you'd experience it as a group: temple walks, street food in Gion, a sake brewery tour, maybe a private tea ceremony.

Spring means the tail end of cherry blossom season or the lush green just after. The city is stunning either way. It's the most "Japan" that Japan gets, and for guests who want temples, gardens, and incredible food, this is the move.

Day 7 Travel to Kyoto, settle in, evening walk through Gion
Day 8 Temples, bamboo grove, group activity (tea ceremony or sake tasting)
Day 9 Free explore, farewell dinner, Nishiki Market
Scenario B

Somewhere New

If you want the second half of the trip to feel completely different, we'd take you somewhere off the tourist trail. Think Tochigi Prefecture (hot spring towns, waterfalls, mountain hiking) or Niigata (rice paddies, craft sake, snow-capped peaks, onsen villages).

These are places most tourists never see. Nature-heavy, slow-paced, deeply Japanese in a way that Kyoto and Tokyo aren't. For a group that values being outdoors and experiencing something unexpected, this is the more adventurous choice.

Day 7 Travel to regional Japan, check into a ryokan or onsen town
Day 8 Hiking, hot springs, local food experiences
Day 9 Explore, farewell dinner at a local izakaya
Day Ten

Departure

Travel back to Tokyo (or fly direct from a regional airport depending on the second city) and head to the airport. Depending on flight times, there may be time for one last meal or a quick stop in Tokyo on the way.

Ten days, one wedding, two cities, and a trip your guests will never stop talking about.
